Title: GA2Graph: Visualizing Online Collaboration Patterns in Computer Science Education

Authors: Abdussalam Alawini, Hajara-Yasmin Isa, Jiabao Xu, Yutong Zhang, and Zhijun Zhao

Published In: FIE 2024

Abstract: This innovative practice full paper describes an approach to visualizing and analyzing collaborative learning. Collaborative learning is vital for enriching computer science education and developing key skills. However, educators often lack tools for effectively tracking and analyzing student en- gagement in group work. Our study introduces GA2Graph, a data-driven system that uses Neo4j to visualize and analyze online collaborative patterns in classroom activities. It graphically represents students and their interactions, helping educators understand group dynamics. We tested this system in a large- enrollment database course, revealing that despite emphasis on assigned roles like those in Process Oriented Guided Inquiry Learning (POGIL), students frequently disregarded them and adopted more individualistic or varied collaborative strategies.

Title: No Wires, Just Wear: Monitoring Breathing With Smart Fabrics

Authors: Hajara-Yasmin Isa; Robin Kravets

Published In: ACM MobiSys 2025

Award: Best Demo Award – Runner Up

Abstract: Wearable sensing technologies offer promising solutions for non-intrusive monitoring in healthcare, fitness, and assistive applications. This paper presents a non-intrusive method for monitoring respiration using an off-the-shelf stretchable conductive fabric that serves as the sensor itself—eliminating the need for embedded components. Real-time breathing patterns are captured by measuring the fabric’s inherent resistance changes during chest expansion and contraction, using a Keysight EDU34450A Digital Multimeter to test the potential of this approach. The system is simple and does not require complex circuit fabrication, making it especially well-suited for continuous monitoring in contexts where comfort and minimal disruption are essential. Preliminary results reveal clearly distinguishable breathing cycles, demonstrating the material’s potential as a wearable and unobtrusive respiratory sensing solution.
