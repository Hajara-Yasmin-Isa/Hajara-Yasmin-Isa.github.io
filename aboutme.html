About Me

Hello! I'm Hajara-Yasmin Isa, a fourth-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science with a focus on interactive computing. Passionate about the intersection of technology and society, my research investigates the application and transformative potential of generative AI.

My journey in tech is enriched by diverse experiences, including my roles as a Heritage Garden Intern Leader, member of the Honors College Advisory Board, Women in Computer Science organization member, Undergraduate Student Mentor, Student Body President, and Black Tech Scholar. This diversity fosters a more inclusive approach to problem-solving in interactive computing.

Committed to increasing representation in tech, I authored a book introducing programming logic and design in Hausa, aimed at providing non-English proficient students with the tools they need to succeed in computer science.

Beyond my academic pursuits, I am known for my curiosity and love for learning. I have a fascination with languages and cultures, enjoy philosophy and psychology, and love archery, ice skating, and Zumba. Additionally, I have a passion for solving riddles and mysteries.
